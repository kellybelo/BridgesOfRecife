Considered the first major bridge in Brazil and the oldest in Latin America, was begun in 1640 by Baltazar Affonseca architect by order of Count Maurice of Nassau, made of wood, and inaugurated on February 28, 1644. Its construction was criticized by the Dutch West Indies Company because it was considered too expensive for a colony.

Completed in just seven weeks in the year 1644, the bridge, originally made from wood, has gone through several reforms, being all iron. The Boa Vista Bridge was acquired from England by the Governor of the Province Henrique de Lucena, who was to become the Baron of Lucena.

Inaugurated in 1863, it was the first bridge in the city made of iron. After a century, the iron structure was replaced by reinforced concrete. Princess Isabel (1846-1921) was the ruler of the Empire of Brazil. Daughter of D. Pedro II, signed the Law which ended slavery in Brazil.
